Hair Extensions Quality - What's the difference

Remy Hair does not always mean virgin hair, in fact it rarely means that. Although the two terms are used synonymously, they are completely different in meanings. Virgin hair refers to hair that is completely unprocessed, and intact. To qualify as virgin hair, it must meet rigorous standards including: not been permed, dyed, colored, bleached, and chemically processed in any way. This also means it comes from a single donor, and all the cuticles are intact, running in the same direction. Virgin hair can last up to a year with proper care and maintenance. When it comes to virgin Indian hair, you have to care for your extensions just like your own natural hair. You need to brush your hair frequently. You need to shampoo and condition your hair regularly, especially if you use a lot of hair products. Deposits from the hair products, if not washed off on a regular basis, can degrade the quality of the hair (just like your own hair) and combined with the use of heat can affect the smooth natural feeling and behavior of the virgin hair. If you maintain your virgin hair extensions properly, you will have sleek, smooth, tangle free beautiful natural looking hair for a very long time and will be able the reuse the hair many times after. Remy Hair is probably the most misunderstood and overused phrase in the hair industry. Remy human hair consists of hair extensions made from virgin human hair of the highest quality grade that has been chemically treated (dyed). Also, all hair strands must follow the same direction and be of the same length to ensure a high quality set free of tangling. Non-remy hair is most popular (knows as human hair) and is readily available as well. This hair has both the roots and the tips mixed up so that all the hairs are not running in the same direction. This type of hair undergoes the shaving off of the cuticles or sometimes the ends of the hair. This reduces the possibility of friction between hair strands and as such allows the hair to remain tangle free as the name suggests. These hairs are all of the same length and are probably the best quality type of non-remy human hair you can find but it is not the best hair extension you can find. Non-remy hair has undergone chemical processing.

Brands

You must decide if you want a branded hair (for example Cinderella, Great Lengths Beauty Works) or non branded hair (hair bought direct from factory) with a brand name you may pay more but you have the benefit of having a company to fall back on should anything go wrong with the hair.

There are many methods of attachment available. Keratin bonded hair, nano rings, taped hair or a weave.

Each option has its pros and cons depending on your expectations and needs.

Weave

The weave is sewn into tiny rings that we attach to your hair at the root (LA Weave method) or we can sew it to a cornrow (classic method) a cornrow is a pleat that we put into your own hair at the scalp, this method is best suited to Afro hair whereas the LA version is best for European hair/scalp.

Weaves come in different lengths from 14 to 22 inches, many colour variations and mixes and different qualities ranging from Human Hair, Remy Hair to Virgin Hair.

Keratin/Protein Bonds

Hair has a keratin tip, Keratin is a protein and the major component in skin, hair and nails. The keratin is heated up using a special hair extension tool and it is rolled or flat bonded onto the hair at the root about an inch from the scalp. This hair usually lasts around three months on average and cannot be reused after it is removed. You don't need to have this hair refitted every 4-6 weeks and when looked after correctly is an excellent Choice.

Keratin/Protein Bond Options

Also known as Flat Bonds, keratin bonds, protein bonds, nail tip bonds & heated bonds.

This is the classic method of attaching hair extensions. A strand of hair usually weighing 0.5 to 1 gram in weight is dipped in keratin, it dries instantly and is wrapped and sold in packs of 25 or 50 bonds. The Extensionist uses a special heat wand to reheat the keratin then attaches the heated bond about a centimetre from the root of your hair by either rolling it or flat bonding it, it cools down instantly and stays in place. This method doesn't need refitted every 6 weeks, which suits many people. I prefer this method for clients who've had extensions before and know how to look after them well. I don't advise this method for extremely fine or processed hair. If you aggressively brush these extensions they can tear your hair from the root so I strongly advise clients who choose this option to choose a high quality hair, this is also a good idea because they last so long in the head that you want hair that will still look and feel good after a few months. A typical full head is anything from 75 to 150 bonds, sometimes people want/need more. Some people like just one r two packs to add fullness to their own hair. The amount needed depends on your own head size, hair length and what look you are going for, natural or full and glamorous. They typically last between 12 to 20 weeks before needing replaced.

Bonds Aftercare

Washing

Do not wash your hair for at least 48 hours after your extensions have been fitted. The bonds need to set and this will help avoid hair shedding in the future. Wash your hair a maximum of twice a week if possible, this will put less stress on your hair. Dry shampoo can be used to freshen hair between washes. Take care when washing and brushing your extensions. When washing it is better to stand in the shower or keep your head upright in the bath when rinsing to avoid tangling or matting. Do not scrub hair on scalp, just gently run fingers between bonds when washing and rinsing. Please note it is better to use shampoos, conditioners and treatments which are designed for hair extension use. Do not use products which contain silicone. For best results after washing and conditioning squeeze the hair with a towel to soak up as much water as possible. Don't put oils onto the extensions.

Brushing and drying

When combing damp hair, always use a wide tooth comb. Support bonds by holding hair mid length. When hair is damp spray in a leave in conditioner. Only use a Tangle Teezer or paddle Brush on hair extensions. Tangle Teezers are available online and in most beauty stores.

Styling

Blow-dry as usual avoiding too much heat on bonded areas this can weaken some bonds depending what brand you choose and can lead to shedding. Style as usual. You can straighten, curl and brush your hair extensions as you would your natural hair. If using straighteners Ect always use heat protector or you will burn the hair. You may notice itching on the scalp when first fitted, this is normal as it takes a little time for the natural hair to adjust. After having hair extensions fitted you may find the hair shedding a little and you may even lose the odd bond; do not panic, anything from 1 to 5 is normal. Keep these safe and I will refit them during your maintenance appointment.

Colouring

It is not advisable to colour hair extensions. Dye contains ingredients which can weaken and break down the bonds. Hair is usually multi-tonal (espicially blonde), dye may pick up different tones and create an undesired finish. If you do wish to dye your hair extensions, always use a hairdresser who has experience/knowledge of extensions. Do not use home dying kits. I cannot be held liable for any problems you may encounter as a result of dying your extensions.

Sleeping

Never go to sleep with wet hair, always make sure your extensions and bonds are dry before sleeping. Sleeping on wet hair can lead to tangling and matting. It is advisable to tie your hair into loose plaits at night, this reduces the risk of tangling.

Holidays/Swimming

It is not advisable to go swimming whilst wearing hair extensions. If you do, try to wear a cap, if possible. Tie your hair on top of your head. DO NOT get extension hair wet. Keep head above water, avoid diving and under water swimming. The chlorine present in pools as well as the salt present in sea along with heat can matt and the chemical within the pool can cause discoloration. Salt, pool chemicals and chlorine can cause the disintegration and break down of keratin bonds which can weakness/stickiness of your extension which can lead to matting or extensions falling out. If extensions get wet while swimming, it is advised that you dry your extensions immediately after rinsing hair out with fresh water and use a leave in conditioner. Avoid brushing wet extension hair and dry them immediately (and its recommended to let them dry out naturally) Make sure that extensions are not tangled after swimming. Again avoid sleeping with wet extensions and ensure that they have been completely dried before going to bed. Wearing your hair extensions abroad can cause dryness of the extension hair so try to keep it nourished. A deep conditioning treatment is recommended upon return.

Micro Rings

The micro ring method is applied using no heat, thread or bonding agent. Using specially designed hair extension strands and rings these extensions really are next to invisible the hair. The ring is put over the top of the strand and squeezed on tight using a special plier type device. This method can be reused but harsh brushing or tugging of the hair can cause them to slide out from time to time, if this happens just keep them and they can be put back in on your next visit to the salon.

Micro Ring Options

A tiny ring (can be aluminium or copper, please state a preference if you have one) is placed over a stick tip/flat bond and clamped shut. These will stay in place but if brushed to aggressively can slip out, they are usually advertised as being kinder to the hair due to the no use of heat, thread or chemicals but in my opinion if looked after correctly no method of hair extension application is damaging. This option is fairly discreet and reusable. They usually need refitted around every 6 weeks. Taped Hair Extensions

Tapes are strips of hair usually about 2 inches or bigger in diameter, the top of the strip of hair has a sticky tape attached, we place this at the root of your hair and we sandwich another strip on top of it to create a seamless undetectable look allowing your own hair to grow out while wearing these extensions. They need retaped with fresh tape every 6 weeks or so and can be reused.

Taped Hair Options

This is 2-inch wefts of hair with a sticky top attached under a section of your hair with another strip placed on top like a sandwich. They look seamless and feel comfortable,but as the hair grows some people complain that the corners can curl up or that the tapes can become more noticeable when the hair is up. A good point is that when tuning your hands over the scalp they can't be felt. They need to be refitted every 4 to 8 weeks in my experience, depending on how quickly your own hair grows. When you come for a refit each tape has to be removed, the old tape should be taken off and replaced with new tape and the natural hair always needs to be washed thoroughly as the tapes leave a sticky residue on the hair. Once washed and dried the retaped hair can be refitted to your hair again. They are reusable but in my experience after 3 refits they can't be used again. It's a quick and easy first fitting but the refit can be a lengthy process. Most people need 3 to 4 packs for a full head although some people get 2 packs for thickness in their already long hair.

General Advice on Extentions


Tip:

I advise my clients NOT to chemically treat their hair extensions, NOT to allow anyone other than a qualified experienced hair extensionist to cut them and to use the shampoo and conditioner I recommend depending on the brand/method you choose. Always use heat protector of you choose to use heat styling tools on your hair. Do NOT use oils on hair extensions or heavy treatments or you can end up with a build up on them that will cause them to become difficult to manage. Going on holiday with extensions is harsh on them and will wear them out quicker. This in my opinion is due to the water you wash your hair with when abroad, being in and out of the pool, the sea, the sun drying them out and even bleaching them plus the atmosphere at night causing them to be difficult.
